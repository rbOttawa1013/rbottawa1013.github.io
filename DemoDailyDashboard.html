Ice Cream and Frozen Desserts: Production, Consumption, and Trade, First Quarter 2026

Released: June 19, 2026

Canadian manufacturers produced 46,326 kilolitres of hard ice cream in the first quarter of 2026, down 8.4% compared with the same quarter in 2025. This production dip coincided with longer-term trends in food availability; although the amount of ice cream available per Canadian rose 5.3% to 4.75 litres in 2023, it remained well below the historical peaks of the 1970s. Meanwhile, annual manufacturing revenues reached $1.4 billion in 2023 (+12.6%), even as processors faced rising operational costs.

Highlights

  • • The amount of ice cream available for consumption per capita in Canada increased by 5.3% in 2023, reaching 4.75 litres per person.
  • • Canadian manufacturers produced 46,326 kilolitres of hard ice cream in the first quarter of 2026, down 8.4% from the 50,602 kilolitres produced in the first quarter of 2025.
  • • Total revenues for the ice cream and frozen dessert manufacturing industry (NAICS 31152) grew 12.6% to $1.4 billion in 2023, up from $1.2 billion in 2022.
  • • The cost of materials and supplies for ice cream manufacturers increased by 18.1% to $903.9 million in 2023, which coincided with a minor decrease in manufacturing value-added to $417.2 million.
  • • Canada imported 10.6 million kilograms of ice cream and other edible ice in 2023, down from the record high of 11.9 million kilograms imported in 2022.

Per capita ice cream availability rises, yet remains below historical peaks

The annual food availability series, which serves as a reliable proxy for consumption by measuring the physical volume of food present in the Canadian market on a per capita basis, indicates a recent uptick in ice cream availability. In 2023, there were 4.75 litres of ice cream available per Canadian, an increase of 5.3% from 4.51 litres in 2022.

To contextualize this annual change:

Percentage Change Formula (2022 to 2023) Percentage Change = ((4.75 L - 4.51 L) / 4.51 L) × 100 ≈ 5.32%

From a long-term historical perspective, ice cream availability has trended downward. Statistics Canada began tracking these data in 1960, when availability stood at 10.47 litres per person. The all-time record high was recorded a decade later, in 1970, at 12.79 litres per person. This long-term downward trend has historically coincided with a growing market share for alternative frozen desserts, evolving nutritional preferences, and changing demographic profiles.

Hard ice cream production reaches 46,326 kilolitres in the first quarter of 2026

During the first quarter of 2026, Canadian dairy processors produced 46,326 kilolitres of hard ice cream. This represents an 8.4% decrease compared with the first quarter of 2025, when production reached 50,602 kilolitres (see Table 1).

Ice cream production is highly seasonal, consistently dipping during the fall and winter quarters before climbing in spring and summer to meet warm-weather demand. For instance, in 2025, production peaked in the second quarter at 61,121 kilolitres before declining to 50,153 kilolitres in the third quarter and further dropping to 35,874 kilolitres in the fourth quarter.

The historical single-month production record occurred in June 1994, when Canadian plants churned out 39,104 kilolitres of hard ice cream. In comparison, monthly production in July 2023 (historically the peak production month of that year) topped out at 17,310 kilolitres.

Manufacturing revenues show robust growth amid rising input costs

The financial performance of Canada’s ice cream and frozen dessert manufacturing industry (NAICS 31152) reflects solid demand alongside elevated operational pressures. In 2023, total revenues for this manufacturing subsector reached $1.4 billion, up 12.6% from the $1.2 billion recorded in 2022 (see Table 2).

However, manufacturers faced significant cost increases. The costs of materials and supplies utilized by ice cream processors rose 18.1% to $903.9 million in 2023, compared with $765.3 million in 2022. Due to these input cost increases, manufacturing value-added decreased slightly from $426.3 million in 2022 to $417.2 million in 2023. The industry intensity ratio—measuring manufacturing value-added as a proportion of total production—dropped from 35.3% in 2022 to 30.7% in 2023.

International trade: Imports stabilize while exports soften

Ice cream and other edible ice products (whether or not containing cocoa) represent a notable segment of Canada's cross-border agricultural trade, primarily integrated with the United States.

Canada imported 10.6 million kilograms of ice cream in 2023. While this is a substantial volume, it fell short of the record high of 11.9 million kilograms imported in 2022. On the other side of the ledger, Canadian exports of ice cream have experienced a long-term downward trend. The largest export volume on record was established in 2006, when Canada exported 29.3 million kilograms. By 2023, total export volume had softened to 9.8 million kilograms.

Definitions and concepts

  • Food availability: Data represent the amount of food physically present in Canada for consumption. It is calculated by adding domestic production to imports, subtracting exports, and adjusting for stock changes. Per capita availability is calculated using population estimates. These data do not measure actual food intake or consumer waste; they are a proxy for food supply.
  • Hard ice cream: Refers to ice cream that is frozen and packaged at the manufacturing facility, typically containing more than 5.0% butterfat by weight.
  • Manufacturing revenues: Include revenues from shipments of goods manufactured, adjusted for changes in inventories, as well as revenue from non-manufacturing activities.
  • Manufacturing value-added: Measures the net output of the manufacturing sector. It is calculated as total manufacturing revenues minus the cost of materials, supplies, fuel, and electricity.

Data on quarterly dairy product volumes (Table 32-10-0483-01) are subject to revision and are not seasonally adjusted. Data on manufacturing activities (Table 16-10-0117-01) are obtained from the Annual Survey of Manufactures and Logging and represent comprehensive enterprise-level reporting.
