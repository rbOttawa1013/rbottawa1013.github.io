A standalone prototype for selecting the right AI prompt pattern by task type, risk level, output use and policy obligation. Use it to create a custom prompt route, before/during/after checklist, prompt starter and print-ready planning guide.

Prototype status: This is a design and planning prototype. It supports responsible prompting practice but does not replace official Statistics Canada, Treasury Board, privacy, security, legal, statistical, communications, official languages, accessibility or information management requirements.

1. Purpose, scope and limits

This guide helps employees convert a work objective into a controlled AI prompt path. The core routing model is:

Task + risk + output use + policy obligations = approved prompt path for human review.

Never route by task type alone. A summary for personal use, a summary for executive advice and a summary for public release require different controls.

Not authorized: This prototype does not authorize the use of AI for classified, protected, personal, confidential, adjudicative, legal, HR, security, financial, statistical-release or decision-impacting work outside approved tools, approved environments and applicable governance processes.

2. Who this guide is for

This guide is for analysts, managers, executives, communications staff, program officers, data and statistical staff, IT and digital teams, researchers, corporate services, and AI community-of-practice members who need a repeatable way to prompt safely and consistently.

5. Prompt paths by task type

Use these cards when you need more context for the route selected by the wizard.

5A. Writing, editing and plain language

Use for rewriting, improving clarity, simplifying, restructuring, inclusive language, plain language and preserving meaning.

Act as a Statistics Canada plain-language editor. Preserve factual meaning. Improve clarity, structure, accessibility and inclusive language. Flag unsupported claims and items requiring human review.
5B. Summarization and synthesis

Use for condensing source material and extracting decisions, themes, risks and actions.

Summarize only the provided source material. Do not add external facts. Separate facts, assumptions, inferences, risks, decisions and action items.
5C. Briefing notes and executive products

Use for executive products, decision context, issue notes, decks and speaking points.

Prepare a concise executive product with issue, context, analysis, options, risks, recommendation, caveats and items requiring human review.
5D. Communications and public content

Use for public, web, intranet, social, media, announcement and employee-facing content.

Draft clear, factual, non-partisan content using only approved facts. Include key messages, accessibility, official languages, disclosure and approval notes.
5E. Policy, program and options analysis

Use for policy questions, program design, implementation options, criteria and recommendations.

Analyze using explicit criteria. Provide options, trade-offs, risks, mitigations, implementation considerations, GBA Plus and Indigenous considerations where relevant.
5F. Data, statistical and evidence analysis

Use for interpreting data, explaining indicators, mapping evidence and reviewing statistical quality.

Interpret only the provided data and source notes. Do not invent figures. Include definitions, quality limitations, caveats and validation items.
5G. Risk, compliance, privacy and security review

Use for issue-spotting, not final expert advice.

Identify possible risks, affected policy areas, missing evidence, mitigations, escalation triggers and required expert reviews. Do not provide final legal, HR, financial, privacy or security advice.
5H. SOP, workflow and process design

Use for repeatable procedures, checklists, RACI and operational controls.

Convert the process into a testable SOP with scope, roles, inputs, outputs, steps, decision points, controls, exceptions, escalation paths, records and audit checks.
5I. Digital, technical, architecture and code prompts

Use for technical drafting, code review, architecture notes, AI workflow design and secure-development support.

Produce or review the technical artifact with security, privacy, accessibility, maintainability, testing, licensing and operational controls. Do not use or expose secrets.
5J. Meeting, consultation and stakeholder planning

Use for agendas, facilitation, engagement planning and follow-up actions.

Create an accessible, inclusive and bilingual-ready meeting or consultation plan with purpose, agenda, roles, participation methods, support needs, risks, records and follow-up actions.
5K. Official languages and bilingual-content support

Use for bilingual drafting support and meaning-equivalence review. It does not replace professional translation where required.

Support meaning-equivalent English/French drafting. Preserve meaning, tone and terminology. Flag uncertain terms and content requiring professional language review.
5L. GBA Plus, inclusion and anti-bias review

Use when the topic affects people, groups, access, equity, representation or services.

Identify possible differential impacts, bias risks, assumptions, excluded perspectives, accessibility issues, official languages considerations and mitigation options.
5M. Indigenous and distinctions-based considerations

Use when content relates to First Nations, Inuit, Métis, Indigenous data, knowledge, communities, lands, rights or programs.

Identify whether distinctions-based review, engagement, data sovereignty, consent or source limitations apply. Do not generalize or speak on behalf of Indigenous peoples or communities.
5N. StatCan publication and statistical-output support

Use for statistical narratives, tables, charts, analytical products and release-readiness review.

Review or draft statistical content using only approved figures and source notes. Include definitions, reference periods, quality caveats, accessibility notes, disclosure/confidentiality flags and validation checks.
6. Detailed guidance

Follow the sections selected by your custom prompt guide, or review all controls when the work is complex, public, statistical, sensitive, operational or high-risk.

6.1 Responsible AI and FASTER controls
What to do
  • Use AI only when relevant to the task and appropriate for the risk.
  • Check fairness, accountability, security, transparency, AI literacy and relevance.
  • Never treat AI output as authoritative without human validation.
6.2 Privacy, confidentiality and protected information
What to do
  • Classify source material before prompting.
  • Do not input personal, sensitive, protected, confidential, secret or classified information into public or unapproved tools.
  • Use data minimization: provide only what is required.
6.3 Security and cyber controls
What to do
  • Remove secrets, keys, credentials, internal endpoints and vulnerable configurations.
  • Require secure coding, testing, peer review and change control for operational use.
  • Escalate cyber-risk issues to the responsible security authority.
6.4 Information management and records
What to do
  • Identify whether the prompt, source material, AI output, validation notes or final product have business value.
  • Record final human decisions and approvals separately from AI drafts.
  • Store approved outputs in the appropriate repository.
6.5 Official languages
What to do
  • Determine whether English and French versions must be available at the same time and of equal quality.
  • Use approved terminology and verify program names, legal wording and statistical terminology.
  • Route formal or public bilingual products for official languages review.
6.6 Accessibility and plain language
What to do
  • Use clear headings, lists, meaningful links, accessible tables and plain language.
  • Do not rely on colour, images, sound, charts or screen captures alone to convey meaning.
  • Check structure, reading order, contrast, alt text, captions, transcripts and file format accessibility.
6.7 Communications, public release and federal identity
What to do
  • Use approved facts, neutral language and non-partisan tone.
  • Check public claims, links, dates, names, numbers and approvals.
  • Apply federal identity, web, accessibility, official languages and disclosure requirements.
6.8 GBA Plus, inclusion and anti-bias
What to do
  • Identify differential impacts, affected groups, assumptions and excluded perspectives.
  • Avoid stereotypes, deficit framing and unsupported generalizations.
  • Validate analysis with human expertise and affected perspectives where appropriate.
6.9 Indigenous and distinctions-based considerations
What to do
  • Use distinctions-based framing for First Nations, Inuit and Métis contexts.
  • Flag Indigenous data sovereignty, consent, source authority and engagement requirements.
  • Do not ask AI to speak on behalf of Indigenous peoples or communities.
6.10 Scientific integrity and evidence standards
What to do
  • Separate evidence, assumptions, methods, interpretation and uncertainty.
  • Use a source hierarchy and disclose limitations.
  • Validate claims with subject-matter expertise before relying on them.
6.11 Statistical quality, disclosure and publication
What to do
  • Use approved data, definitions, reference periods, methodology and quality notes.
  • Do not invent numbers, denominators, methods, comparisons or confidentiality statements.
  • Complete validation, disclosure/confidentiality, methodology, communications, OL and accessibility checks before release.
6.12 AI-use disclosure and approvals
What to do
  • Track whether AI substantively contributed to drafting, analysis, coding, summarization, translation support or publication content.
  • Apply departmental disclosure procedure where required.
  • Notify the appropriate manager or reviewer when AI use is material to the product.
6.13 Procurement, vendor, licensing and intellectual property
What to do
  • Check whether the AI tool, model, vendor service or software dependency is approved for the intended use.
  • Review terms of use, licensing, copyright and reuse restrictions.
  • Do not assume ownership, publication rights or open-source compatibility without review.
